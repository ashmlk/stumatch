Cookies

A cookie is a small piece of data stored on your computer while browsing the web. Cookies are used to identify you and store and receive information will you visit different websites. Cookies have various uses, such as authentication, keeping content you already entered in other fields, etc.

When you use Corscope we use your cookies. We use cookies only for authentication and safety purposes only. We do not use your cookies when you visit other webpages.

This page explains how we use your cookies. Our other data collection policy is described in our Data Policy section.

Why do we use cookies?

Cookies help use to provide a safe browsing experience for our users and protect their accounts.

  • Authentication: We use cookies to determine if a user is logged in so we can make it easier for them to access different pages across our website.
  • Security and Integrity: Cookies help us to determine whether a user is attempting to access Corscope without authorization. Cookies also allow us to prevent phishing attacks by helping us identify devices that have opened the number a large number of accounts in a short period.
Where do we use cookies?

We place cookies on your device when you visit our website pages. Please note that our only purpose of using cookies is to authenticate and offer security to users.

Who has access to your cookies?

We do not allow any third party app to use your cookies on our website.

Third parties may use cookies on their sites and apps. To better understand their policies, please review their policy.
